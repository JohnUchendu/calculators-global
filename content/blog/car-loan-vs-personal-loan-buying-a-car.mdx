When buying a car, most people focus on the vehicle price — not the financing cost. But the difference between a car loan and a personal loan can add up to thousands over the life of the loan. Here is how to decide which is cheaper for your situation.

## What is a car loan?

A car loan (also called auto financing or hire purchase) is a secured loan where the vehicle itself is the collateral. Because the lender has security, rates are typically lower than personal loans.

## What is a personal loan for a car?

A personal loan is unsecured borrowing. You receive the funds, buy the car outright, and repay the loan in fixed instalments. No lien is placed on the vehicle.

## Key differences

| Factor | Car loan | Personal loan |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| Security | Vehicle | None |
| Typical rate (US) | 5%–9% | 10%–20% |
| Typical rate (UK) | 6%–12% | 7%–15% |
| Typical rate (Nigeria) | 22%–28% | 26%–32% |
| Ownership | Lender until paid | Yours immediately |
| Flexibility | Car-specific | Buy from anywhere |
| Approval speed | Moderate | Fast |

## Real numbers — $20,000 car over 5 years

**Car loan at 7%:**
- Monthly payment: ~$396
- Total interest: ~$3,741
- Total repaid: ~$23,741

**Personal loan at 13%:**
- Monthly payment: ~$455
- Total interest: ~$7,317
- Total repaid: ~$27,317

The car loan saves $3,576 in interest over 5 years. That is meaningful — but not always the deciding factor.

## When a personal loan makes more sense

- You want to buy from a private seller (dealer financing not available)
- You want to own the car outright with no lender lien
- You have excellent credit and qualify for a low personal loan rate
- The car is older and lenders won't finance it
- You want to pay it off quickly without early repayment penalties

## The total cost is what matters

Always compare the total amount repaid — not just the monthly payment. A longer loan term reduces monthly payments but increases total interest. Run both scenarios through the calculators before deciding.

## Frequently asked questions

**Does a car loan affect my credit score?**
Yes — like any loan, timely payments improve your credit score and missed payments damage it. A car loan is a standard instalment credit product.

**Can I pay off a car loan early?**
Most car loans allow early repayment, but some charge an early settlement fee. Check your agreement before making extra payments.

**Is it better to finance through a dealer or a bank?**
Dealers sometimes offer promotional rates (0% for a limited period) that are genuinely competitive — but may build the cost into the vehicle price. Always negotiate the car price separately from the financing.
